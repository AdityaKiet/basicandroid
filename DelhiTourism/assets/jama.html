Jama Masjid

Jama MasjidJama Masjid is one of the largest mosques in India, also known by the name of "Masjid-i-Jahan Numa" (visible to the world). Situated near the Red Fort in Delhi, it is one of the last monuments built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan.One of the most valued treasures of the Delhi Jama Masjid are a hair of the beard of Hazrat Mahmmad.
Hazrat Mahmmad used chappal (footwear), the canopy of his tombstone, the foot print of Muhammad on the stone and a chapter of Koran taken from its original holy book. All of these are kept in the northeast corner of the mosque.
